Conduct at Church Activities

Church activities should be uplifting and should help participants draw closer to Jesus Christ. All participants are expected to follow Church standards during activities, whether or not they are members of the Church.

Those who participate in Church activities should:

  • Follow Church standards of conduct, including the Word of Wisdom, the law of chastity, and other principles of righteous living.
  • Refrain from the use of language or conduct that is vulgar, crude, or demeaning to others.
  • Respect the property of others and of the Church.
  • Refrain from the use of alcohol, tobacco, vaping, and drugs, and from the misuse of any substance.
  • Refrain from possessing or using weapons, fireworks, or other dangerous materials.
  • Comply with all applicable laws.

Leaders should explain these standards to participants before the activity begins. Those who violate these standards will be sent home at their own expense and may not be allowed to participate in future activities.

Reporting Abuse: If a participant becomes aware of any abuse, neglect, or other conduct that may endanger others, he or she should report it immediately to a leader, parent, or other trusted adult. Leaders should follow the instructions in the Church's abuse help line and report to local authorities as required by law.

Two-Deep Leadership: At least two responsible adult leaders (age 21 or older) should be present at all activities involving youth. Both male and female leaders should be present at activities that include both young men and young women. A leader should never be alone with a youth.

Safety: Leaders should ensure that activities are conducted safely. They should be aware of potential hazards and take steps to prevent injuries. Leaders should be prepared to respond to emergencies and should have a plan for contacting emergency services. Leaders should also be aware of the medical conditions of participants and should have access to the medical information provided on this form.

By signing this form, I acknowledge that I have read, understand, and agree to abide by the standards of conduct described above during the activity. I understand that failure to comply with these standards may result in my being sent home from the activity at my own expense.
